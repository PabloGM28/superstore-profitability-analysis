# Superstore Profitability Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the profitability of a retail business using the Superstore dataset.  
The business shows strong sales performance, but profitability is underperforming in specific regions, states, product categories, and discount levels.

The analysis was performed entirely in Excel and presented through a PowerPoint business deck.

---

## Business Question

**Why is the business generating strong sales but low profitability?**

---

## Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Calculated fields
- Charts
- PowerPoint

---

## Dataset

The analysis is based on the Superstore dataset from Kaggle.

Dataset: Sample Superstore  
Source: Kaggle

---

## Methodology

The analysis followed a structured business analytics approach:

1. Analyzed profitability by region
2. Identified underperforming regions
3. Analyzed high-revenue states within those regions
4. Compared sales performance against profit margin
5. Reviewed customer segments
6. Analyzed profitability by product category and sub-category
7. Evaluated the impact of discounts on profit margin
8. Assessed discount dependency in loss-making states
9. Developed business recommendations

---

## Key Findings

- Strong sales performance is not translating into profitability.
- Central and South regions concentrate the lowest margins.
- Several high-revenue states show negative margins.
- Customer segment does not explain the profitability issue.
- Furniture significantly underperforms compared to other categories.
- Losses are concentrated in specific sub-categories such as Tables and Bookcases.
- Profitability declines sharply as discount levels increase.
- Discounts above 20% generate negative margins.
- Loss-making states are fully dependent on discount-driven sales of at least 10%.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Enforce stricter discount controls, especially above 10–20% thresholds.
- Redesign pricing strategies in loss-making states to reduce discount dependency.
- Prioritize high-margin sub-categories without aggressive discounting.
- Implement discount optimization models to balance volume and profitability.

---

## Final Conclusion

Profitability can be significantly improved by reducing structural dependence on discount-driven sales and implementing more disciplined pricing strategies.
